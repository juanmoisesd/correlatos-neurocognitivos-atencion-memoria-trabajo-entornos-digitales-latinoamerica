## 1. Title
Mindfulness and the Sentimental Brain: Neurobiological Mechanisms of Training for Inner Peace

## 2. Abstract
Mindfulness—non-judgmental awareness of the present moment—is a validated neurobiological intervention. This manuscript analyzes how training reshapes the "sentimental brain." We investigate remodeling across four domains: (1) the Default Mode Network (DMN), facilitating a shift from rumination to presence; (2) the Frontolimbic Circuitry, reducing amygdala reactivity; (3) the Attentional Network, increasing cortical thickness in the ACC; and (4) the Interoceptive System (insula). By synthesizing voxel-based morphometry (VBM) and functional connectivity data, we demonstrate that regular meditation induces significant neuroplasticity. We conclude that mindfulness promotes resilience and emotional clarity, justifying its integration into public health policy.

## 3. Keywords
Mindfulness, meditation, neuroplasticity, emotional regulation, cognitive neuroscience.

## 4. Introduction
High-resolution neuroimaging reveals that sitting in silence physically changes the brain. Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) has shown efficacy in treating anxiety and depression. This review clarifies the biological "how" behind these psychological changes, moving the brain from reactive survival to reflective peace.

## 5. Theoretical Framework
- **DMN Silencing:** Reducing over-activity associated with self-criticism and temporal travel (PMC, 2022).
- **Executive Brake:** Enhancing the PFC's ability to inhibit amygdala hyper-arousal.
- **Interoceptive Awareness:** Strengthening the insula to detect emotional signals early.

## 6. Methodology
Systematic review of neuroscientific literature (2008-2024), focusing on longitudinal RCTs utilizing MRI and fMRI with at least 8 weeks of practice.

## 7. Results
Mindfulness training significantly lowers baseline cortisol. Expert practitioners show a "decoupling" of pain sensation (thalamus) from emotional suffering (PFC), allowing for calm in stressful circumstances.

## 8. Discussion
The concept of "Mental Hygiene": training attention to prevent neural dysregulation. Limits of research: self-selection bias and the "dark side" of meditation in rare cases. Building cognitive reserve.

## 9. Conclusions
Mindfulness is biological empowerment. It is a fundamental pillar of health that fosters long-term emotional stability. Future research should explore "Digital Mindfulness" to support the brain's capacity for presence.
