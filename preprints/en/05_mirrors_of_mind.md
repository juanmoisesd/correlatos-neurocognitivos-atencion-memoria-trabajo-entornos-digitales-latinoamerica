## 1. Title
Mirrors of the Mind: A Multidimensional Analysis of the Mirror Neuron System and Intersubjectivity

## 2. Abstract
The discovery of mirror neurons provided the first biological framework for human intersubjectivity. This manuscript reviews the Mirror Neuron System (MNS), analyzing its role in action understanding, intention detection, and affective resonance. We explore the "embodied simulation" hypothesis, which posits that the brain understands others by simulating their states within its own motor and sensory networks. The study discusses the distinction between the MNS (automatic resonance) and the Theory of Mind network (cognitive perspective-taking). Findings indicate that MNS integrity is essential for social cohesion and imitation-based learning. We conclude that mirror neurons represent the biological engine of empathy.

## 3. Keywords
Mirror neurons, social neuroscience, empathy, intersubjectivity, embodied simulation.

## 4. Introduction
Humans are wired for connection. The Mirror Neuron System, discovered by Rizzolatti and team, suggests that our brain simulates the actions and emotions of others to understand them. This paper bridge the gap between cellular mechanisms and high-level social behavior.

## 5. Theoretical Framework
- **Embodied Simulation:** Vittorio Gallese (2001) proposed that we "grasp" the meaning of others' actions through direct neural mapping.
- **The Insula Link:** Mirroring of emotional expressions translates into felt states via the insula station.
- **Social Contagion:** The MNS drives the automatic imitation of gestures and affects that builds rapport.

## 6. Methodology
Integrative review of primary research on the MNS (1992-2024), including single-unit recording in primates and human fMRI studies.

## 7. Results
Mirror neurons fire according to the *goal* of an action, not just the physical movement. Individuals with higher empathy scores show more intense MNS activation during social tasks.

## 8. Discussion
We discuss the "Broken Mirror" theory of Autism and its recent refinements. The limits of reductionism: empathy is more than just mirror neurons; it requires prefrontal integration.

## 9. Conclusions
The MNS demonstrates that the barrier between "self" and "other" is biologically porous. Empathy is a neural muscle developed through interaction.
